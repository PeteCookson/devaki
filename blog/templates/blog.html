The 5 principles of Yoga explained

Post by Lorna Fox, Oct 23, 2021.

What are the five principles and how can they help us? The five principles of yoga are proper relaxation, proper exercise, proper breathing, a proper diet and positive thinking and mediation. It's almost like thinking of our body as a car - we need to rest our car, but we also need to drive it, we need to ensure we put the right fuel into our car and we need to make sure we carry out a regular service to our car, ensuring it stays positive and on the road. The five principles do the same. Proper relaxation ensures we release tensions in the muscles and rests our whole system, helping us to be refreshed - a bit like after a really good night's sleep. Proper relaxation can carry on through our daily life, helping us know when to let go of worries or fears. In our Sivananda practise, we punctuate our asanas with proper relaxation and end our sessions with a deep, final relaxation - for some, this is the best part of our practise! Proper exercise is our yoga postures, or asanas. They work systematically on all sections of our body, stretching, flexing and toning the muscles and ligaments, keeping the spine and joints flexible and improving the circulation. Proper breathing is helped by our pranayama and means breathing fully and rhythmically, making use of all of our lungs to intake oxygen. Pranayama, yogic breathing, helps to recharge your body, calm the mind and regulate the flow of prana - the life force. A proper diet, ideally the sattvic diet, is well-balanced and nourishing and is based on natural, non-stimulating foods. It keeps the body light, the mind calm and gives resistance to disease. It also helps in our flexibility and our strength. As our Swami wisely said in the ashram - "at mealtime, fill your stomach half full with food, a quarter with water and leave the other quarter for gas"! Find out more about the sattvic diet in my other blog. And finally, positive thinking and meditation - two powerful practises that help to remove negative thoughts, still the mind and support us to observe the world around us - bringing us to a place of calm and inner peace.
