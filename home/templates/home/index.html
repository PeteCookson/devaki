Why Yoga?

Anyone can practise yoga. You don’t need special equipment or clothes – just a small space, a mat and a strong desire for a healthier, more fulfilled way of life. Yoga postures, or asanas, exercise every part of the body stretching and toning the muscles, joints, as well as the skeletal system, working on the glands, nerves and internal organs, keeping all systems aligned and in radiant health. The yogic breathing exercises, pranayama, revitalise the body and focus the mind, leaving you calm and refreshed, whilst positive thinking and meditation give clarity and concentration.

About Sivananda

Yoga is regarded as a divine science of life, revealed to enlightened sages in meditation. The oldest evidence of yoga was found in a number of stone figures showing yogic postures, dating back to 3,000 BC. One of the most important figures in the recent development of yoga has been Swami Sivananda. Originally a medical doctor, he decided to follow the spiritual path, writing over 300 books, pamphlets and leaflets, bringing his medical history to his yoga teachings. Swami Sivananda founded Sivananda yoga which grew to what it is today, with many established ashrams and Yoga Academies across the world.
